Medieval France

843 – Treaty of Verdun

Divided Charlemagne’s empire into West Francia, East Francia, and Middle Francia, laying the foundation for modern France and Germany.

1337–1453 – Hundred Years’ War

France and England battled over succession. Joan of Arc's involvement gave rise to French nationalism and helped crown Charles VII.

1534 – French Colonial Expansion

Explorer Jacques Cartier laid claim to Canadian territories for France, marking the beginning of French influence in North America.

1598 – Edict of Nantes

King Henry IV enacted the Edict to ensure religious tolerance for Huguenots, ending decades of religious wars and fostering national unity.
