French Revolution

1789 – Storming of the Bastille

The fall of the Bastille prison marked the start of the French Revolution and symbolized the collapse of royal tyranny.

1791 – Constitutional Monarchy

The National Assembly adopted the Constitution of 1791, limiting the power of the king and establishing a legislative assembly.

1792 – France Becomes a Republic

The monarchy was officially abolished and France declared a republic following Louis XVI’s suspension.

1793–1794 – Reign of Terror

Radicals led by Robespierre initiated mass executions of perceived enemies of the revolution, including King Louis XVI and Queen Marie Antoinette.

1799 – Napoleon’s Coup d’État

Napoleon Bonaparte overthrew the Directory and established the Consulate, ending the French Revolution and rising to power.
