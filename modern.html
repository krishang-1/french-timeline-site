Modern France (1940–Today)

1940–1944 – Nazi Occupation

France was occupied by Nazi Germany during WWII. Resistance movements and the Allied liberation ended the occupation.

1958 – Fifth Republic Established

Charles de Gaulle founded the Fifth Republic with a stronger presidency and new constitution for modern French governance.

1962 – Algerian Independence

After a violent decolonization war, France granted independence to Algeria, ending a major chapter of French colonialism.

1992 – Maastricht Treaty

France ratified the treaty forming the European Union, committing to greater European economic and political integration.
